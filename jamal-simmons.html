Jamal Simmons- Professional Career

Jamal began his professional career as an advisor to Max Cleland while working for Carolyn Cheeks Kilpatrick. Simmons began working as a press assistant on Bill Clinton’s 1992 presidential campaign 1992. He devoted two years of his life (1993-1995) to the United States Trade Representative’s office, where he worked as a press assistant. Later, he was hired as a special assistant to Commerce Secretary Mickey Kantor for two years in a row. He also worked in the scheduling and advance office.

Later, he departed the Clinton administration and began working at Citigroup and United Technologies, positions he could only hold for a short time. Simmons worked as the chief of staff for Congresswoman Carolyn Cheeks Kilpatrick in 1999 and 2000, as well as the deputy communications director for the AI Gore 2000 presidential campaign. Later, he became the Democratic National Committee’s director of communications, as well as the communications director for Max Cleland’s re-election campaign in 2002.

Jamal Simmons then served as the press secretary for Bob Graham’s 2004 presidential campaign, as the travel pass secretary for Wesley Clark’s 2004 presidential campaign, as a senior vice president at the Walker Marchant Group, and finally as the founder of his consultancy firm. He rose to the position of principal in the Raben Group. He has appeared as a political pundit on CBS, NPR, CNN, MSNBC, and The Hill, among others. Washington Life Magazine named Simmons one of the most important persons under the age of 40 in 2009.
