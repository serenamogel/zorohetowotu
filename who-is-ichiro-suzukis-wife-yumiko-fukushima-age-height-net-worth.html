Often referred to as the Japanese Michael Jordan ofBaseball by his fans, Ichiro Suzuki is undoubtedly one of the greatest Japanese players to have played this game. Beyond his legendary baseball status, Ichiro is widely considered a national institution in his home country of Japan. He played in Japan for the first nine years, after which he made the intercontinental transfer to American MLB team Seattle Mariners in 2001. This career-defining decision saw the left-handed player was the first Japanese non-passer to be signed by a team. the United States.

Since 2001, Ichiro has remained a leader and created several records during his long professional career. Ichiro Suzuki aka “Hit the Machine” had his best streak in 2004 breaking an 84-year-old record for most hits in a single season by ending the year with a record 262 hits. The historic game in which this new record was set was described by Suzuki as the finest moment of his career and also earned him a frenzied following from fans.

Ichiro Suzuki Biography, Age

Ichiro Suzuki was born in Kasugai, Aichi on October 22, 1973, to Yoshie and Nobuyuki Suzuki. However, he was raised in Toyoyama City, Aichi with his only brother, a sister named Kazuyasu Suzuki. His father, Nobuyuki Suzuki, was a high school baseball player in his prime. So he was determined to give his son an early start in the sport. His father started giving him lessons when he was three years old.

Ichiro received his high school education at AikodaiMeiden High School, a school renowned for its renowned baseball program. The young player was recruited as a pitcher in the school’s baseball program, which marked the start of one of the most illustrious individual sports careers in Japanese history. Ichiro posted impressive stats late in his high school career with a .505 cumulative batting average and 19 home runs.

Ichiro Suzuki was drafted by the Orix BlueWave in the November 1991 draft of Nippon Professional Baseball in Japan. His nine years with the team have been rewarded with some of the most prestigious awards in Japanese baseball, including 7 star elements, 3 MVP (Most Valuable Player) awards, 7 Golden Glove awards and 3 unprecedented grand prizes in Japanese professional sports..

Ichiro Suzuki joined the Seattle Mariners from Major League Baseball in 2001 where he spent most of his 12-year career in MLB. In July 2012, the talented player joined the New York Yankees where he spent two and a half seasons and later signed with the Miami Marlins. Ichiro Suzuki returned to his first MLB team, the Mariners, in 2018 after a 3 season season with the Marlins.

Ichiro Suzuki currently holds the MLB record for most hits in a season (262), most successive seasons with 200 hits (10), and also most hits by a player of Japanese descent. As an intercontinental star, Ichiro also holds the record for most hits by a single player in top-tier baseball leagues in the world. He was inducted into the Japanese Baseball Hall of Fame, becoming the first MLB player to be so honored.

Who is Ichiro Suzuki’s wife?

Just like his brilliant sports career, IchiroSuzuki also had a good performance at home. The legendary baseball player is married to his wife Yumiko Fukushima, a former sportswoman. The couple tied the knot on December 3, 1999 in Santa Monica, California. Yumiko Fukushima also manages her husband’s finances. Early in his career, Ichiro’s finances were handled by his father. However, he handed over the crucial role to his wife following a tax evasion scandal that cost the baseball star thousands of dollars, in addition to intense media scrutiny.

Net value

Ichiro Suzuki belongs to the rank of privileged players who have conquered more than one top league in their professional career. In addition to his career earnings, the international baseball star also enjoys several juicy endorsement deals in the United States, his home country, Japan, and around the world. Ichiro’s net worth is estimated at $180 million.

Height and other body measurements

The baseball superstar stands at a height of 5 feet 11 inches (180 cm) with a body weight of 79 kg (175 lbs). By baseball standards, Ichiro is relatively small. However, whatever the Japanese-born star lacks, he adequately makes up for in strength and stamina.
